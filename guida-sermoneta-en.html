Visiting Sermoneta: A Guide to the Medieval Village

Sermoneta is an untouched gem, a dive into the Middle Ages just a few kilometers from the sea and Rome. With its imposing castle, ancient walls, and cobbled alleys, this village offers an authentic and fascinating experience. From our B&B, the historic center is easily accessible, making us the perfect base for your exploration.

The Caetani Castle: The Heart of the Village

Dominating the landscape, the Caetani Castle is the symbol of Sermoneta. Built in the 13th century, it is one of the best-preserved fortresses in Lazio. The guided tour is a must to discover its halls, prisons, and ramparts, which offer a breathtaking view of the Pontine plain.

  • Tickets and Hours: Updated information is available on the Roffredo Caetani Foundation website, which also manages the castle.
  • Events: Throughout the year, the castle hosts historical reenactments, exhibitions, and concerts. Check the calendar before your visit!

Getting Lost in the Alleys: What Not to Miss

The best way to discover Sermoneta is to walk without a specific destination. However, there are some spots that are worth a visit:

  • Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta: A beautiful example of Romanesque-Gothic architecture with an impressive bell tower.
  • Loggia dei Mercanti: A charming 15th-century portico, once the bustling heart of the town's trade.
  • Jewish Synagogue: A testament to the vibrant Jewish community that inhabited the village in the Middle Ages.
  • Belvedere: A panoramic viewpoint from which to admire a spectacular sunset.
