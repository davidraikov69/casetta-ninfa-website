Visiting Norma: The Balcony over the Pontine Plain

Perched on the Lepini Mountains, Norma is a surprising destination, famous for its spectacular panorama, ancient ruins, and an unexpected sweetness. It's a perfect day trip to combine with a visit to Sermoneta and Ninfa, easily reachable from our B&B.

The Archaeological Site of Ancient Norba

A short walk from the modern center lie the impressive ruins of Norba, an ancient Latin city destroyed in 81 B.C. The archaeological area is freely accessible, and walking among the cyclopean walls, thermal baths, and temple remains with the Pontine plain at your feet is a unique experience.

  • Access: Free and open.
  • Tip: The best time to visit is at sunset, when the light paints the ruins and the view becomes magical.

The Chocolate Museum: A Sweet Surprise

Norma is also known for its connection to chocolate. The **Antica Norba Chocolate Museum** offers an interesting journey through the history of the "food of the gods" and, most importantly, the chance to taste and buy high-quality artisanal products. A delicious stop that will delight both adults and children.

The Flight of the Peregrine Falcon and Paragliding

Thanks to its location, Norma is a landmark for free-flight enthusiasts. It's not uncommon to see the colorful sails of paragliders and hang-gliders dancing in the sky, taking advantage of the updrafts. Even just watching them from the belvedere is a fascinating spectacle.
